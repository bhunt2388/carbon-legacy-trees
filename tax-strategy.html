THE ANCHOR PLAY — §168(k)(5)

Specified Plant Election: 100% Deduction the Year You Plant

Under the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (signed July 4, 2025), Congress permanently restored 100% bonus depreciation under §168(k). For investors in Carbon Legacy Trees, this unlocks a specific election that changes the entire return profile of the investment.

THE LEGAL BASIS

What Is a "Specified Plant"?

IRC §168(k)(5)(B) defines a specified plant as: "(i) any tree or vine which bears fruits or nuts, and (ii) any other plant which will have more than one crop or yield."

Carbon Legacy Trees are regenerative. The root system survives every harvest and produces 15 harvest cycles per tree. By statute, any plant producing more than one yield qualifies as a specified plant.

Confirmed: 15-cycle regenerative harvest satisfies the §168(k)(5)(B)(ii) multi-yield requirement. CPA-verified.
HOW THE ELECTION WORKS

Deduction in Year of Planting

1

Trees are planted on project land (all 2025–2026 plantings qualify).

2

Investor's CPA attaches a §168(k)(5) election statement to the timely-filed tax return for the planting year.

3

100% of the tree planting cost is deducted in the year of planting — not depreciated over years. Full deduction, Year 1.

4

Trees continue growing, generating carbon credits, lumber, and graphene — with zero remaining depreciation basis to recapture.
