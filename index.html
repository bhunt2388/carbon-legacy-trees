Lifecycle Economics

15 Harvests — One Permanent Planting

Unlike annual crops or single-harvest timber, Phoenix Trees regenerate after each harvest. The root system remains intact — providing self-watering, ongoing carbon sequestration, and rapid regrowth between cycles.

🌳

Plant Once. Harvest 15 Times.

After a Phoenix Tree is harvested, the root system — now deep enough to access groundwater independently — drives rapid regeneration. Each subsequent harvest cycle draws on a more established root network. The result: costs stay fixed at planting while revenue compounds across 15 harvests plus continuous annual carbon credit income throughout the entire lifecycle.

The Process

From Bare Land to
Four Revenue Streams

Carbon Legacy Trees manages every step — planting, irrigation, registry certification, harvest coordination, and credit sales. Partners provide land or capital. CLT provides everything else.

01
Site Assessment & Partner Agreement
CLT evaluates land characteristics, soil composition, water depth, and sun exposure. For investors, deal structure and return waterfall are finalized. For land partners, revenue share terms are set. All agreements include exclusive rights to all four revenue streams from that acreage.
02
Planting & Establishment
Phoenix Trees are planted at CLT's optimized density. Initial irrigation is active for Years 0–2. Carbon sequestration begins immediately upon planting. Registry baseline measurements are logged for future credit certification. Carbon credits are generated from Day 1.
03
Annual Carbon Credit Issuance
Each year, CLT submits verified sequestration data to registered voluntary carbon market registries. Credits are certified, serialized, and made available for sale to corporate buyers and tax credit purchasers. Carbon revenue is distributed per agreement terms.
04
Harvest & Regeneration
At maturity, CLT coordinates harvest of dense Phoenix hardwood. Lumber is sold at premium structural rates. A portion of harvested material goes through the combustion/graphene pathway. Revenue is distributed. Trees regenerate from the established root system — cycle repeats up to 15 times.
